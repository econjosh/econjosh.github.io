I am a Lecturer/Assistant Professor at the University of St Andrews Business School. In Spring 2025, I visited Bank for International Settlements (BIS) as a Central Bank Research Fellow. Previously, I was a Senior Research Economist at the Center for Excellence in Finance and Economic Research (CEFER) at the Bank of Lithuania and a Research Affiliate at the School of Economics and Business at Kaunas University of Technology (KTU).

In 2020, I graduated with a PhD in Economics from the University of Washington. My research focuses on international macroeconomics and trade, with a particular emphasis on the policy implications of globalization and deglobalization.
