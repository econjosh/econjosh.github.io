Working Papers

The Short-Run vs. Long-Run Impact of Tariffs with Variable Markups, and FDI
(Nov 2022)

This paper studies the impact of ad valorem tariff in an environment that features firm heterogeneity, variable markups (as in Melitz and Ottaviano [2008]), and FDI (as in Helpman et al. [2004]).The analysis of the findings shows: (1) The presence of FDI generates a competitive effect on the economy, and firms need to be more productive to survive in the domestic market. (2) The presence of ad valorem tariff and quadratic quasi-linear preference collectively result in an endogenous level of firm entry. Therefore, a tariff change entails quite different implications for the short run (restricted firm entry) vs. the long run (free firm entry). (3) Markups also respond to tariff change differently in the short run vs. the long run, primarily due to the change of the competitive environment associated with the firm entry.

Optimal Tariff with Firm Heterogeneity, Variable Markups, and FDI
Under Review, also available as Bank of Lithuania Working Paper No 99.

Variable markups and multinational production have gathered considerable attention in the trade literature because of their empirical prevalence and welfare implications. This paper studies the welfare implication of tariffs and optimal tariffs in an environment that features firm heterogeneity, variable markups, and FDI. I find: (i) Tariffs endogenously affect firm entry level, producing different comparative statics in the short-run versus long-run. (ii) Variable markups generate multiple externalities in this economy, causing market outcome to differ from the socially optimum outcome systematically. Permitting tariff-jumping FDI can lower the domestic cutoff levels and reduces the misallocation in the economy. (iii) Free trade is not always socially optimal. If the domestic marginal cost cutoff is suÿciently high, a positive tariff can be welfare-improving since it encourages firm entry. The Nash equilibrium tariff level will also be higher than the socially optimal tariff. (iv) The interaction of variable markup and FDI generates novel welfare implications that are absent if consumers possess CES preference.

Firm Heterogeneity, Variable Markups, and Multinational Production: A Review from Trade Policy Perspective
( Journal of Economic Surveys 36(5), 1311-1357 (2022), also available as Bank of Lithuania Working Paper No 92 )

This paper surveys the main ingredients and results of heterogeneous firms trade policy literature that has been developing since the early 2000s. First, I present the stylized facts regarding firm heterogeneity, firm-level markups, and multinational production’s global structure. I then survey the trade policy papers that build on the workhorse model of firm heterogeneity. Third, I summarize the recent development of theoretical approaches of modeling the firm-level markups and its trade policy implication. Fourth, I discuss the theoretical frameworks that incorporate multinational production into heterogeneous firms’ framework and their trade policy implication. Finally, I discuss directions for future research and offer suggestions for further readings.
